Crew 1 Slpashes Down

Sunday, May 2, 2021

Astronauts Mike Hopkins, Victor Glover, Shannon Walker, and Soichi Noguchi returned to Earth last night after a 6 month stay on board the International Space Station. Crew Dragon Resilience undocked from the station at about 8:37 PM EST on Saturday night. After several departure burns from the station, dragon moved to perform its 16 minute long deorbit burn. Following that, around 2:45 AM EST on Sunday morning, dragon re-entered the Earth's atmosphere. Just under 15 minutes later, Resilience splashed down off the coast of Panama City, Florida.

This mission made history is many ways. Crew 1 was the first operational mission of Crew Dragon as part of the commercial crew program. It also featured the first international partner on board a private spacecraft from Soichi Noguchi of the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA). Finally, this was also the longest mission in United States space program history at 168 days.

NASA Announces Crew 1 Return Date

Yesterday, NASA announced the date of the return of the SpaceX Crew 1 Mission. Teams are currently targeting April 28, 2021 for the return of Crew 1. The mission launched in November 2020 to the ISS carrying NASA astronauts Mike Hopkins, Victor Glover, Shannon Walker, and JAXA astronaut Soichi Noguchi. Splashdown is targeted for around 12:35 PM EST on April 28, weather dependent. Teams will also choose one of several available landing zones off the coast of Florida once the date gets closer in order to evaluate a primary landing zone depending on weather and recovery availability.
