SN15 Lands!

Wednesday, May 5, 2021

On Wednesday, May 5, SpaceX's Starship SN15 successfully lifted off from suborbital pad A from the Starbase launch site in south Texas. SN15 is the first full scale starship prototype outfitted with a nose cone and multiple raptors to successfully perform a soft landing. As some of you may know, Starship SN10 did land after its flight, however due to a hard landing, it exploded a few minutes after touching down. At this point, Starship is standing verticle on the landing pad waiting for teams to move in and secure the vehicle. However, it is unclear what SN15's fate is from this point on. The most likely scenario would be for SpaceX to disassemble SN15 to learn about how the vehicle performed and what condition it is in after flight. Other possible scenarios include an inspection of the exterior of the vehicle, and it is simply retired, or teams could put SN15 back on the pad for a reflight. Check back here for updates on SN15's future as well as when we could see SN16 fly.


SN15 Flight Scrubbed

Tuesday, May 4, 2021

After moving in to final flight preparations, SpaceX decided to call off SN15's 10 kilometer flight for Tuesday, May 4. It is unknown why the flight was scrubbed, however teams do have a back up opportunity tomorrow, May 5. There is currently a TFR scheduled for 7:00 AM EST through 8:00 PM EST as well as a road closure from 1:00 PM EST through 9:00 PM EST and marine hazard zones posted for tomorrow, May 5. We are currently waiting for village evacuation notices, and official confirmation from SpaceX. Make sure to check out our new Live Updates page for updates on when we recieve confirmation of these requirements.


SN15 Flight Delayed... Again

Sunday, May 2, 2021

Similaryly to Friday, the temporary flight restriction for Monday, May 3, 2021 for SN15's flight was cancelled. The current NET date for SN15's 10km flight is now Tuesday, May 4, 2021. According to the current TFR's, the window lasts from 7:00 AM EST through 8:00 PM EST. It is unknown why the TFR was cancelled, however it was likely due to forecasted weather including high winds throughout most of the planned window. We are still waiting on road closures, marine hazard zones, village evacuation notices, and official confirmation from SpaceX. Make sure to check out our brand new Live Updates page for updates on when we recieve confirmation of these requirements.


SN15 Flight Delayed

Friday, April 30, 2021

The temporary flight restriction (TFR) for Saturday, May 1, 2021 for starship SN15's 10km flight was removed from the FAA's website. The current NET date is currently Monday, May 3, 2021 with a TFR already in place. Teams will continue to watch the weather for Sunday. Still awaiting: road closures, marine hazard zones, village evacuation notices, and official confirmation from SpaceX.
